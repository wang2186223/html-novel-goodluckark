Chapter 42

How Did You Know My Size

The soft heat of Alexander’s breath ghosted over the delicate skin behind Evelyn’s ear.

A violent shiver racked her body instantly.

It was just a whisper of warmth, yet it sparked an electric current deep within her.

Every nerve ending tingled in its wake.

Her heart hammered against her ribs, a frantic, heavy rhythm.

Her throat constricted, unbearably tight and dry.

A sudden jolt of alarm shot through her.

Her eyes flew open wide in pure shock.

Instinct took over.

She jerked back from his powerful frame, putting space between them.

Her breath caught as she stared at him, utterly disbelieving.

His physical limitations were known, but the sheer control he exuded was undeniable.

If he wished it, he could still command the very air around them.

This realization sent another wave of shivers through her—a mix of awe and deep unease.

She dropped her gaze quickly, refusing to meet his eyes.

She was afraid of what she might see there.

Alexander’s intense stare never wavered.

He studied every micro-expression that flickered across her face.

His lips curved into a slow, deliberate smile.

A mischievous gleam darkened his gaze.

“Why are you looking at me like that?” His voice was like smooth velvet. “Do I resemble a monster to you?”

The exaggerated question made her flinch involuntarily.

She shook her head rapidly, wanting to erase the harsh image. “No,” she stated firmly.

She hesitated, her lips parting slightly.

“It’s just… you pulled me so close. So suddenly. We were… I’m not accustomed to such proximity.”

Alexander’s gaze softened noticeably.

His voice dropped to a low, sincere murmur. “My apologies. I did not intend to frighten you.”

Evelyn started to shake her head, to dismiss the moment.

But he let out a soft, low chuckle.

With casual ease, he added, “But we are legally married now, Evelyn. You will need to grow accustomed to this kind of closeness. Don’t you agree?”

His words were gentle, yet they carried a significant, unspoken weight.

It felt like a promise of something inevitable.

Then, his tone shifted, becoming lighter. “Take a look. Does this suit me?”

Evelyn paused, then allowed her eyes to lift.

He sat before her, clad in the new outfit she had chosen.

The moment her eyes settled on him, pure admiration washed over her features.

She had deliberated for what felt like an eternity selecting these pieces.

Seeing him wear them now, the effect was breathtaking.

The style was a departure from his usual formal wear, yet it fit him impeccably.

It looked as though it had been tailor-made just for him.

The casual aesthetic made him seem more accessible, more vividly human.

Alexander adjusted his position slightly, his eyes watching her intently.

“Your silence is concerning. Does it look bad?” He fiddled with the shirt collar, a rare show of uncertainty.

Evelyn couldn’t suppress a genuine smile.

“No,” she said, her voice filled with earnest appreciation. “It looks absolutely incredible on you!”

A proud smile touched her lips. “It seems my eye for style is perfectly accurate.”

Alexander chuckled, amused by her rapid shift from flustered shyness to confident self-praise.

“Indeed, your taste is exceptional.” His voice was soft, his eyes tracing her every move.

After a thoughtful moment, he raised a curious eyebrow.

“How did you know my size so precisely?”

The clothes fit him without a single alteration needed.

Evelyn’s smile widened, her tone turning playful with growing confidence.

“I studied fashion design. I have a very good understanding of measurements and proportions.”

Alexander blinked, genuinely taken aback. “You studied fashion design?” he asked, surprise evident in his voice.

Evelyn was on the verge of explaining further—that she didn’t just study it, but worked at the very company where Alexander’s corporation still held a substantial debt.

Before she could form the words, his phone emitted a sharp beep, signaling a new message.

Immediately, Evelyn fell silent.

Her eyes remained on him as he gave her a brief, acknowledging glance and reached for his device.

As Alexander read the message, the relaxed expression vanished from his face.

His brows drew together in a sharp line of sudden, deep concern.
