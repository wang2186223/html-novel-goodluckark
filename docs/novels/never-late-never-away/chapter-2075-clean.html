Ever since they came back, Caiden never once went back home. He was either busy with his coding or racking his brains to set up a chance encounter with Joan every day.

“I’m rather busy these days, so I’ve got no time to go back home,” Caiden replied tersely beside him.

Busy? What’s he so busy about every day? He’s got a large-scale business in his family, yet he isn’t interested in taking over. Instead, he insists on doing some lame coding! I really can’t figure out what he’s thinking! Jaden heaved a sigh.

Oh wow, he’s really angry! It looks like Joan Watts is indeed an effective threat!

He had lived with his grandparents ever since young, so his parents were never present during his childhood. It was only when his grandparents passed away did he went back home. They were good friends who grew up together, so he understood Caiden’s circumstances. Hence, he always put up with his occasional outbursts, no matter how bad they were.

He knew that Caiden wasn’t malicious at heart but simply rather obstinate at times. And that was also why he had always been worried that he would be hung up on Joan.

Good God, he has truly lost his mind! It’s none of my business? What has he been taking me for throughout the years?

Sometimes, he really wished he could just lay into Caiden. Not only did he always go off tangent, but he also had a bad temper.

Slam! The bang of a door slamming shut had Caiden jolting.

The cell phone beside him was ringing incessantly, but he showed no signs of answering it. He remained buried in his work as though everything else in this world, save coding, had nothing to do with him.

“Good God, can you please pick up the call, Caiden Owens? It’s really driving me out of my mind! Your mother keeps calling me, asking whether you’d gone missing or fallen sick! In fact, she’s calling even more frequently than my mother…” Jaden groused in a near shout.
