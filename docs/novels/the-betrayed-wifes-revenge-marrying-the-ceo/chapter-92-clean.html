I observed Catherine and Molly nervously approaching my office.

After receiving my call, these two women had delayed a full twenty minutes before appearing. Standing before me now, they deliberately maintained some distance from each other, as if trying to dissociate themselves.

Catherine's face was noticeably pale, her fingers constantly twisting together; Molly's gaze darted around, avoidingdirect eye contact with me.Seeing their nervous demeanor, I nearly laughed out loud but maintained my expressionless facade.

I casually set down the documents on my desk and addressed them in a deliberately controlled tone: "There's a new game project I'd like to assign to both of you. I specifically called you here to ask..."

I paused meaningfully, noting how Catherine's shoulders visibly tensed and Molly held her breath. Then I continued,"...if you have the capacity to take on this responsibility."

My tone remained strictly professional, without any indicaton that I was aware of their prank.

Catherine appeared momentarily stunned by this unexpected turn.She blinked rapidly, her lips parting slightly before closing again. Molly glanced toward Catherine, uncertain how to respond,her fingers nervously fidgeting with the hem of her clothing."Well?" I prompted simply. "We'd be happy to take it on!" Catherine responded eagerly without hesitation. Her voice suddenly rose by about eight pitches, her shoulders visibly relaxing.

I calmly continued, "I've already spoken with the department head. You can meet with him directly on the twentieth floor."

"That's great!" Both women noticeably relaxed, the tension leaving their faces as they excitedly left my office.

I watched them go, finally allowing myself a small satisfied smile.

Everything was proceeding according to my expectations.

*Dare to play a prank on me?*

Sarah got straight to the point: "I want to talk to you about Annie Baker."Her voice was deliberately gentle, but the calculation in her eyes was unmistakable.
