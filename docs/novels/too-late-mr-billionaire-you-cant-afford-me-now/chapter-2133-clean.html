As if it were no great matter, Eric spoke of how he had sent Elissa and Locke off to the Scott family’s remote private island. Still, Hadley understood that for most people, such a plan bordered on impossible.

While Eric’s support had made a difference, it wasn’t the main issue. More than anything, the weight of having lied to Ernest was eating away at Eric. Sharing news of Addy’s condition with Elissa right now might drive her to drop everything and race back to Srixby.

Should that happen, their efforts to help her escape would unravel, leaving Elissa vulnerable to Ernest once again, with no way out of his reach. Worse yet, Eric’s involvement would come to light, making it impossible for him to face Ernest afterward.

Trust, once broken, would make it unthinkable for Ernest to forgive the two people he had relied on most—Eric and Hadley.

The idea haunted Hadley, and she feared it might forever damage the bond between Eric and Ernest.

One look at her troubled expression told Eric everything he needed to know.

Nothing escaped him; whatever crossed Hadley’s mind, he was already thinking as well. But even in that moment, Eric found a sense of comfort knowing Hadley cared about him, too. That reassurance meant the world to him. Any sacrifice felt worthwhile if it was for her sake.

In a gentle voice, he said, “You don’t have to think about me. Whenever you’re ready, I can have someone inform Elissa right away…”

“No, don’t!” A quick shake of Hadley’s head stopped him. Conflicted thoughts warred inside her. “Maybe things won’t turn out as badly as we fear.”

Hearing her say that brought a gentle flutter of hope to Eric’s chest.

“Let’s not worry just yet.” Eric reached out, smoothing her hair with care. “Maybe Addy will pull through. Whatever happens next, I trust your decision.”
