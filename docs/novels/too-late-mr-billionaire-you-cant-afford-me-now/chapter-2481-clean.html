“Got it.” Brady gave a quick nod and walked away.

Now the room felt empty. Hadley picked up the glass of water from the table and took small sips, every now and then glancing out at the peaceful garden.

In that moment, she stopped short. Eric had appeared, quietly standing right in front of her. It wasn’t surprising to see him there. Naturally, Louise’s birthday meant the Hayes family would invite every famous name in town.

Eric, however, had shown up well before the crowd.

For a moment, he stood there in complete silence. Hadley wasn’t sure what to make of it. Not wanting to linger on whatever he was thinking, she shifted her attention away, picked up her own glass of water, and grabbed Brady’s as well. She stood, deciding to move somewhere else for a bit. With so much space in the room, if Eric wanted that particular seat, she had no problem leaving it for him.

Hadley stepped forward, then took another cautious stride.

Eric didn’t budge. He remained silent, almost frozen, as each second ticked by. Without warning, he pivoted and started after her. In a low, steady voice, he called out, “Hadley Pearson!”

Hadley flinched. This was the first time in five months she’d heard him say her full name. A strange and subtle feeling stirred inside her—something she couldn’t quite put into words. Meeting Eric’s gaze, she realized something about him felt… off. She kept quiet, waiting.

Her nerves coiled tight, Hadley rushed out of the room.

“Wait up!” Eric’s voice rang out as he hurried after her.

“Hadley! There’s something I need to tell you!”
